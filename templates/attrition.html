Attrition and Salary Insights

Understanding the relationship between attrition rates and employee salaries is crucial for enhancing retention strategies.

Attrition vs Salary

This chart compares attrition rates with employee salaries, providing insights into how compensation affects retention.

Analysis

The attrition vs salary graph illustrates that employees with higher salaries tend to have lower attrition rates. This suggests that competitive compensation is a key factor in employee retention. Notably, departments with significant variations in salary also show differing attrition trends, which could indicate the need for targeted salary reviews to address potential retention issues.

Salary Distribution

This graph shows the distribution of salaries within the company, helping to identify variations in pay scales across different roles.

Analysis

The salary distribution graph reveals how salaries are spread across different positions within the company. Analyzing this data helps management understand where inequities may exist and allows for necessary adjustments to retain top talent and maintain overall employee satisfaction.

Key Insights

- Higher salaries correlate with lower attrition rates.
- Significant salary disparities exist among departments, warranting review.
- Continuous analysis of salary and attrition data is vital for effective retention strategies.
